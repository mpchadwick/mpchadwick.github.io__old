About

Me and my dog, Diana, after a nice long walk in Central Park

1985

Ten days overdue, and after putting my Mom through a hellish twenty hour labor, on August 17th I was born at Northern Westchester Hospital in Mount Kisco, New York.

1995

My parents bought me a stratocaster for my birthday and I began taking guitar lessons with a teacher named John, who was really into blues rock.

1998

I began playing in my first band, called Wired, with a group of three other friends. We mainly played original rock music songs which we sometimes wrote together and sometimes individually. One memorable song was called "Farm", and its subject was the daily routine of a farmer who "had too much to do".

2001

I downloaded FruityLoops (now FL Studio) onto my computer and stayed up late into the night producing ambient electronic beats. There were tracks with names such as "Sonic Screwdriver" and "What Is Happening To Me".

2003

In June I graduated from Horace Greeley High School in Chappaqua, New York. I had particularly excelled in the math and sciences area, getting a 5 on the AP calculus exam. Later, in September I entered Northeastern University's Music Technology program to pursue my love of electronic music composition.

2007

I travelled to the United Kingdom for 3 months from September to November during a semester off. During that time I met the person that would become my wife.

2008

I graduated from Northeastern University in Boston, Massachusetts with a Bachelor of Science in Music Technology. I also got my first taste of web design during my final semester in an introductory graphic design course.

Later, after graduation, I moved to Japan and found a job as an English teacher at the American Language School.

2011

After the Great Tohoku Earthquake, my wife (at that time fiancee) and I moved back to New York. Shortly after I began working at CCM Marketing Communications, a small, boutique-y New York City-based full service marketing agency.

Originally hired as an account coordinator, after a few months at the agency, So I turned to resources like The New Boston and open courseware from iTunes U and taught my self basic, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP and MySQL.

2012

In February, I launched my first major website project, which was redesign of the agency's old flash site. In the same year, under the direction of the agency, I also launched the Fu-Ki Sake Desktop and Mobile Site, an e-commerce site for Sazerac Company, and a promotional sweepstakes microsite for Little Hug.

In July, my wife and I adopted Diana, a rescue pit bull from the NYACC on 110th Street in Harlem

2013

I have continued my studies on modern web technologies, and begun to learn about version control, MVC frameworks, CSS pre-processors and Test Driven Development.

On August 30th my wife gave birth to our beautiful daughter Sally.
